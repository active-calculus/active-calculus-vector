Recall that we call an oriented curve \(C\) closed if it has the same initial and terminal point. A typical example of a closed curve would be a circle (with orientation), but we could also consider something like the square with vertices \((1,1)\text{,}\) \((-1,1)\text{,}\) \((-1,-1)\text{,}\) and \((1,-1)\text{,}\) oriented clockwise (or counterclockwise).
