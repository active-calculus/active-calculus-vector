Fundamental Theorem of Calculus for Line Integrals Let \(f\) be a function for which \(\nabla f\) is continuous on a domain \(D\text{.}\) If \(P\) and \(Q\) are points in \(D\) and \(C\) is a piece-wise smooth oriented path from \(P\) to \(Q\) in \(D\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C \nabla f\cdot d\vr = f(Q) - f(P)\text{.} \end{equation*}
