If an oriented curve \(C\) ends at the same point where it started, we say that \(C\) is closed. The line integral of a vector field \(\vF\) along a closed curve \(C\) is called the circulation of \(\vF\) around \(C\text{.}\) To emphasize the fact that \(C\) is closed, we sometimes write \(\oint_C \vF\cdot d\vr\) for \(\int_C \vF\cdot d\vr\text{.}\) Circulation servies as a measure of a vector field's tendency to rotate in a manner consistent with the orientation of the curve.
