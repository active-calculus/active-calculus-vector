(If \(\vF\) is a vector field in \(\R^2\text{,}\) the \(F_3\, dz\) term is omitted.) As a concrete example, if \(\vF(x,y,z) = \langle x^2y,z^3,x\cos(z)\rangle\) and \(C\) is some oriented curve in \(\R^3\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \int_C\vF\cdot d\vr = \int_C x^2y\, dx + z^3\, dy + x\cos(z)\, dz\text{.} \end{equation*}
